Overview

What is QUEST-PCC?

QUEST-PCC fills a critical gap in volumetric media research. Unlike existing human-centric datasets, it captures the full diversity of real-world 3D content, enabling content-aware compression and streaming research.

🎯
Content-Diverse

Six object categories — Vehicle, People, Architecture, Artifact, Animal, and Furniture — covering rigid, articulated, and non-human geometries rarely found in existing datasets.

🗜️
MPEG V-PCC Encoded

All sequences encoded with the MPEG TMC2 v25.0 reference software using five standardized rate configurations (r1–r5), enabling reproducible and fair rate–distortion comparison.

📊
Rich Quality Metrics

Per-frame MPEG PCC metrics: MSE Point-to-Point, PSNR Point-to-Point, and RGB PSNR (R, G, B channels). Fully computed and bundled — no re-encoding needed.

🔁
Fully Reproducible

Encoding scripts, configuration files, and evaluation pipelines are released alongside data, enabling researchers to extend, adapt, or verify results independently.

🌐
Streaming-Ready

Bitrate ladders from sub-1 Mbps to ~80 Mbps are designed to match realistic adaptive streaming operating ranges for immersive 6DoF volumetric media delivery.

🔓
Open Source

Released under the Apache 2.0 License. Free for academic and commercial use, redistribution, and modification — with proper attribution.

Applications

Where is QUEST-PCC Useful?

Designed for systems-level research on volumetric media — not perceptual studies. QUEST-PCC is the right benchmark for the following research areas.

📡
Adaptive Volumetric Streaming

Build and evaluate DASH/RTP-based adaptive bitrate algorithms for 6DoF point cloud delivery, leveraging realistic content-dependent bitrate ladders spanning sub-1 Mbps to 80 Mbps.

🧠
Content-Aware Encoding

Study how object topology, motion complexity, and surface geometry drive encoding bitrate — enabling smarter, content-adaptive encoder parameter selection beyond traditional one-size-fits-all configurations.

📈
Bitrate Ladder Construction

Design per-content or per-category bitrate ladders. The dataset's content diversity reveals how ladder spacing must adapt to structural complexity and motion dynamics for each object class.

🔬
Rate–Distortion Analysis

Conduct systematic RD analysis using MPEG-standardized P2P and PSNR metrics. Investigate how geometry fidelity and attribute distortion correlate across object categories and quality levels.

🕹️
Immersive XR & 6DoF Systems

Benchmark VR, AR, and mixed reality delivery pipelines for dynamic volumetric content. Validate V3C codec profiles against non-human objects that challenge standard human-centric models.

⚙️
Cross-Layer Optimization

Explore joint encoding, network, and rendering optimizations. QUEST-PCC provides the data backbone for DRL-guided, QUIC-aware, or MoQ-based volumetric media delivery experiments.

Key Findings

Content-Dependent Rate–Distortion

Through sequence- and category-level analysis, we show that encoding bitrate and reconstruction quality are governed primarily by dynamic surface topology and structural complexity — not point count alone. Sequences like Gorilla and Dancing Man exhibit dense, irregular geometry that fragments V-PCC atlas patches, dramatically inflating bitrate compared to geometrically simple objects like Gyroscope.

PSNR Point-to-Point (Avg) Geometry fidelity across sequences. Bitrate gaps between successive quality levels follow approximately logarithmic spacing (1.5–2× step increases).
MSE Point-to-Point (Avg) Mean Squared Error across categories confirms monotonically decreasing geometric distortion as quality level increases from r1 to r5.

Topology Drives Bitrate

Atlas packing efficiency — shaped by object surface topology and V3C patch generation — is a stronger bitrate predictor than point count.

Motion Inflates Cost

Articulated motion (Dancing Man, Gorilla) causes fragmented patches and high spatial entropy, yielding disproportionately high HEVC intra-frame costs.

Geometry Outweighs Motion

Wardrobe, despite limited motion, requires high bitrate due to dense geometry and occlusion-heavy structure — geometry complexity can outweigh motion.

Logarithmic Bitrate Spacing

Successive quality levels produce approximately 1.5–2× bitrate steps, forming realistic ladders for adaptive streaming across all content types.
